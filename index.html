About This Textbook

Heat and mass transfer textbooks tend to be comprehensive but impersonal. This one makes a different bet: that students learn faster when the physics is tied to something they can hold in their hands.

The 3D Printer as a Classroom

A filament deposition printer is the perfect teaching object. Conduction runs up the nozzle from the heater block. Convection strips heat from each deposited bead as air moves past. The heated bed radiates energy to the cooler room. All three modes operate simultaneously, and every one can be calculated with equations introduced in this course.

Later chapters extend the analogy to injection molding, extrusion dies, membrane separations, and nanopore systems.

How to Use This Textbook

  1. Work through chapters in order. Each one builds on the last.
  2. Read the narrative before looking at equations. Physical understanding makes the math easier to remember.
  3. Use hints when stuck. They are staged: direction first, then equation, then setup.
  4. When a question is wrong, a variant appears before the answer. Try it.
  5. Open the AI tutor for anything the text does not address. It runs locally on your machine.

Prerequisites: Introductory calculus, differential equations, and one semester of thermodynamics.
