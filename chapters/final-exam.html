Part A — Short Answer (Conceptual) 10 points each × 5 questions = 50 pts
C1 Ch. 1 & 11 — Heat–Mass Analogy

Write Fourier’s law and Fick’s first law from memory, side by side. Identify the analogous quantities (flux, driving force, transport property) in each. A polymer membrane separates two regions with different solvent concentrations. Explain in two sentences why the same mathematical machinery used to compute heat flux through a composite wall can be applied directly to compute solvent flux through a layered membrane.

C2 Ch. 8 — Transient Conduction

Write the lumped capacitance equation from memory (the expression for $T(t)$) and define every symbol. (a) Derive the time constant $\tau$ from the energy balance $\rho c_p V\,dT/dt = -hA(T-T_\infty)$. (b) A polymer pellet ($\rho = 1000$ kg/m3, $c_p = 2000$ J/kg·K, $L_c = 2$ mm) is cooled with $h = 100$ W/m2·K. Calculate $\tau$ and verify that Bi $= hL_c/k < 0.1$ for $k = 0.25$ W/m·K.

C3 Ch. 13 & 14 — Membrane Mass Transfer

Write the solution-diffusion model: state the relationship $P = D \times S$ and define all symbols. (a) A membrane has permeability $P_\text{O2} = 2P_\text{N2}$ but diffusivity $D_\text{O2} = D_\text{N2}$. Using the $P = DS$ framework, determine the selectivity ratio $S_\text{O2}/S_\text{N2}$. (b) Explain how you would modify the polymer to increase $D$ for both gases simultaneously, and what trade-off this introduces for selectivity.

C4 Ch. 9 & 10 — Forced vs. Natural Convection

Write Newton’s law of cooling. Explain the physical origin of the heat transfer coefficient $h$ in forced convection versus natural convection. For a polymer cooling on a mold, state which mode of convection delivers higher $h$ and explain why. In a forced-convection situation where $\text{Re} = 10^5$ and $\text{Pr} = 5$, would you expect the thermal boundary layer to be thicker or thinner than the velocity boundary layer? Justify using Pr.

C5 Ch. 15 — Coupled Heat & Mass Transfer

In a polymer film drying process, heat must be supplied to evaporate solvent while solvent vapor must diffuse out through the film. Write the governing equation for transient mass diffusion (Fick’s second law) from memory. Explain what the Lewis number Le $= \alpha/D$ tells you about the relative rates of heat and mass transport. If Le $\gg 1$, which process is rate-limiting in drying, and what design strategy follows from this?
