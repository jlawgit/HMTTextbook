Part A — Short Answer (Conceptual) 10 points each × 5 questions = 50 pts
C1 Type 1 • Ch. 1 — Fourier’s Law

Write Fourier’s law of heat conduction and explain the meaning of each symbol. What does the negative sign in the equation represent physically? Explain why metals have thermal conductivities $k$ several orders of magnitude higher than typical polymers, using a molecular-level argument.

C2 Type 1 • Ch. 1 — Newton’s Law of Cooling

State Newton’s law of cooling. A process engineer doubles the temperature difference $\Delta T$ between a polymer sheet and the cooling air, while keeping everything else constant. By what factor does the heat removal rate change? Now suppose instead the engineer doubles the heat transfer coefficient $h$ at constant $\Delta T$. Compare the two cases and explain which change is easier to achieve in practice.

C3 Type 2 • Ch. 2 — Thermal Resistance

Write the expression for thermal resistance per unit area $R''$ for a flat wall of thickness $L$ and conductivity $k$. A two-layer mold wall consists of Layer 1 (polymer, $k_1 = 0.20$ W/m·K, $L_1 = 20$ mm) and Layer 2 (steel, $k_2 = 15$ W/m·K, $L_2 = 5$ mm). Without performing the final division, calculate $R_1''$ and $R_2''$ and explain which layer controls the overall heat transfer rate and why.

C4 Type 2 • Ch. 4 — Cylindrical Resistance & Critical Radius

Write the formula for the thermal resistance of a cylindrical shell (per unit length, or total for length $L$). Does a critical radius of insulation exist for a flat wall? Explain clearly why the critical-radius phenomenon is specific to geometries where surface area changes with insulation thickness, and what happens to heat loss as insulation outer radius increases beyond $r_\text{cr}$.

C5 Type 3 • Ch. 3 — Heat Generation

A polymer slab of half-thickness $L$ with both surfaces cooled to $T_w$ generates heat uniformly at rate $\dot{q}$ (W/m³). (a) Write the steady-state governing equation. (b) State the boundary conditions. (c) Derive the expression for $T_\text{max}$ (the centerline temperature). (d) If the heat generation rate is doubled, by what factor does the temperature rise $T_\text{max} - T_w$ increase?

Part B — Calculation Problems 10 points each × 5 problems = 50 pts
P1 Type 1 • Fourier’s Law — Flat Wall

A PLA part cools on a heated build plate. The PLA layer acts as a flat wall with thermal conductivity $k = 0.13$ W/m·K and thickness $L = 26$ mm. The bottom surface (touching the plate) is at 190°C and the top surface (exposed) is at 40°C. Calculate the steady-state heat flux $q$ conducted through the PLA layer.

Work
$q =$ W/m²
P2 Type 1 • Newton’s Law of Cooling

A polymer injection-molded part has a flat surface area of $A = 0.050$ m². It is cooled by forced air with heat transfer coefficient $h = 20$ W/m²·K. The part surface temperature is 100°C and the cooling air is at 40°C. Calculate the heat removal rate $Q$ from the part surface.

Work
$Q =$ W
P3 Type 1 • Radiation — Stefan-Boltzmann

A heated ABS surface (emissivity $\varepsilon = 0.90$) is at $T_s = 373$ K (100°C) and faces surroundings at $T_\text{surr} = 293$ K (20°C). Calculate the net radiation heat flux $q$ from the surface. ($\sigma$ provided above.)

Work
$q =$ W/m²
P4 Type 2 • Series Thermal Resistance

A composite insulating panel has two layers in series: Layer A ($R_A'' = 0.20$ m²·K/W) and Layer B ($R_B'' = 0.10$ m²·K/W). The temperature on the hot face is 130°C and on the cold face is 40°C. Calculate the steady-state heat flux $q$ through the panel.

Work
$q =$ W/m²
